Minecraft Server Rules

Information Dates

Effective Date: June 7, 2026

Last Updated: June 7, 2026

1. General Rules

  • No hacking, hacked clients, or game-breaking exploits.
  • No griefing or stealing from other players' builds.
  • Avoid lag-heavy builds (massive redstone farms, entity spam). Check with staff if unsure.
  • Inactivity without notice may get your access reviewed. Let staff know if you're away.

2. PvP & Conflict

  • PvP is allowed. If you engage, you accept retaliation until both parties publicly agree to peace.
  • Collateral damage to uninvolved players or builds is punishable regardless of who started it.
  • Breaking an agreed peace is bad faith. The victim's retaliation remains fully protected. Staff only intervenes if escalation occurs or court is requested.

3. Vigilante & Coalition Law

  • Vigilantes have no official protection. You intervene at your own risk.
  • Proportionate good-faith intervention may receive leniency in court — not guaranteed.
  • Causing collateral damage while intervening makes you liable regardless of your intentions.
  • Players may form coalitions against aggressors, including those abusing war declarations.

4. War Declaration Law

Based on international law — Hague Convention 1907 & UN Charter
  • Self-defence — no declaration needed. If attacked first, retaliate immediately.
  • Offensive war — must be publicly announced in Discord before hostilities begin. No surprise wars.
  • The accused may respond — accept, negotiate, surrender, or fight back. Silence = refusal to negotiate.
  • Any player may ally either side or remain neutral.
  • War ends via mutual peace treaty or surrender — both must be publicly announced in Discord.
  • War crimes apply during war — collateral damage to uninvolved players or builds remains punishable.
  • Executives act as peacekeeping force (UN-style) — they do not take sides but intervene on war crimes or formal court requests.

5. Property & Damage Law

  • Looting a death corpse without prior consent is theft and court-eligible.
  • Pre-agreed PvP loot terms stated before combat are binding and exempt from theft rules.
  • Creeper grief — if a creeper damages another player's property due to your negligence or intent, you are liable for repairs within 24 hours of the incident. If you were not notified within 24 hours, the deadline resets to 24 hours from notification. Evidence required for a formal case — otherwise social sanction only.

6. Punishment System

Punishments are based on severity. Staff may apply one or more:

  • Warning — formal notice on record
  • Kick — removed from server session
  • Financial Sanction — 5,000 IDR paid to server fund
  • Inventory + XP Wipe — all items and experience wiped
  • Temporary Ban — suspended for a set period
  • Permanent Ban — removed from DevenSMP permanently
  • Victim Compensation — Perpetrators may be required to refund lost gear, resources, or fulfill negotiated terms as ruled by staff.

7. Court System

  • Any player may formally dispute an incident by submitting their case to an available Executive.
  • The Executive acts as arbitrator. Rulings are final.
  • Both parties must provide evidence. Cases without sufficient proof may be dismissed.
  • Executives recuse themselves if personally involved in the dispute.
